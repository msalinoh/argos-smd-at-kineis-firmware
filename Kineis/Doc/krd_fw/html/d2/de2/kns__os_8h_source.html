9 /**
10  * @page kns_os_page OS abstraction layer for Kinéis

12  * This page is presenting the Kineis OS design (\ref KNS_OS, \ref KNS_Q)
13  *
14  * @section kns_os_intro Introduction
15  *
16  * As on lots of OS, queues are used by kineis SW environement (kineis stack + application) to
17  * share events and data between different tasks/ISR contexts. Source SW entity (task) pushes
18  * event+data into the queue from one context (e.g. ISR) and the destination layer (task) will pop
19  * it from its own context.
20  *
21  * @section kns_os_list List of tasks
22  *
23  * Kineis OS does not assume any priorities between tasks directly. But it is done through the side
24  * effect of priorities declared on queues. An event delivered in a high-priority queues shall be
25  * consumed as soon as possible by the dedicated task.
26  *
27  * Below is typical list of tasks imvolved in Kineis FW environement (kineis satck + application):
28  * * KNS_OS_TASK_APP one application task defined by default. User can decide to add an extra
29  * application task through (\ref kns_os_conf.h) configuration file. Refer also to
30  * \ref kns_os_conf_page for details.
31  * * KNS_OS_TASK_MAC Kineis stack main task
32  * * KNS_OS_TASK_IDLE idle tsak entering LPM when other task are not active
33  *
34  * @section kns_os_subpages Sub-pages
35  *
36  * * @subpage kns_os_conf_page
37  * * @subpage kns_q_page
38  * * @subpage kns_q_conf_page
39  */
